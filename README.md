## 📋 What is Vespera

Vespera acts as a companion for your favorite music players. It runs alongside your music software to provide extra features that standard players often lack. This tool connects to your system to control playback and display information in real time. It works well on different versions of Linux. By using this tool, you get better control over audio settings and visual elements while you listen to your tracks.

## 🛠 Features

Vespera offers several tools to improve your listening habits:

*   **MPRIS Control:** You control your music playback through a standard interface. This allows you to pause, skip, and manage tracks from one place.
*   **Synced Lyrics:** The software fetches song lyrics as you listen. The text scrolls in time with the music.
*   **Cava Visualizer:** A visual element moves to the beat of your songs. This light show responds to the frequency of the audio output.
*   **EasyEffects Integration:** You gain access to an equalizer. You adjust sound profiles to fit your speakers or headphones. 
*   **Wayland Support:** The application works on modern desktop display systems.

## ⚙️ Initial Configuration

After you open Vespera, the application scans your system for compatible music players. If the application does not find your music player, ensure your player supports the MPRIS standard. Most modern music applications include this feature by default.

To adjust sound quality, navigate to the equalizer tab within the Vespera window. You see several sliders. Move these sliders up or down to change the bass, mid-range, or treble levels. You save your custom settings as a personal profile for future use.

## 📊 Using the Visualizer

The Cava visualizer turns on automatically when you play music. You decide how it looks by changing the settings in the Visualizer menu. You choose the color of the bars, the height of the waves, and the animation speed. If the visualizer misses the beat, check your audio input source in the settings menu to ensure Vespera listens to the correct output device.

## 🎤 Managing Lyrics

Vespera searches for lyrics based on the metadata found in your music files. Metadata includes the artist name, song title, and album title. If the data is missing, the lyrics window shows no text. To fix this, use a tagging tool to update the information in your music files. Once the files have correct tags, Vespera displays the lyrics as you play the track.

## 🛡 Security and Privacy

Vespera interacts only with your local music player and audio drivers. The application does not send your music library information to external servers. It does not monitor your private files. The visualizer and equalizer tools process audio data only while the application stays active. Close the window to stop these background processes.

## 🔧 Troubleshooting

If you encounter issues, check these common items:

*   **No sound:** Verify that the output device in your system settings matches the source Vespera uses.
*   **Visualizer frozen:** Restart the application to reset the visual engine.
*   **Missing lyrics:** Ensure your songs have correct tags. Check your network connection to allow the app to fetch text from online databases.
*   **Equalizer unresponsive:** Confirm the EasyEffects service runs in the background. Vespera relies on this service to modify the audio signal.

If the problem persists, visit the issues section on our GitHub page to log a report. Provide details about your Linux version and the music player you use.

## 📝 Usage Tips

Place Vespera in a small window at the corner of your screen to monitor your music without distracting you from your other tasks. You pin the window to the top of your other programs if you prefer the lyrics or the visualizer to remain visible at all times. Use keyboard shortcuts to toggle the equalizer settings if you switch between different types of audio content, such as podcasts or music.
